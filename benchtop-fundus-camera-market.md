<p><p>The benchtop fundus camera market is categorized into three main types: mydriatic, non-mydriatic, and hybrid cameras. Mydriatic cameras require pupil dilation to obtain high-resolution retinal images, making them useful for detailed examinations. Non-mydriatic cameras do not necessitate dilation, offering convenience for quick screenings and patient comfort. Hybrid cameras combine both functionalities, allowing flexibility in imaging without compromising on image quality. Each type caters to different clinical needs, enhancing ocular diagnostics in various healthcare settings.</p></p>
